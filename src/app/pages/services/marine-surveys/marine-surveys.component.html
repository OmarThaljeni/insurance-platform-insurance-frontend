Marine Surveys & Inspections

Professional survey services across all Tunisian ports — protecting shipowners and their P&I clubs.

Cargo Surveying

Investigating all types of cargo to ascertain damage, shortage and causes — on board, at quay, or at receivers' premises.

  • All cargo types: bulk, liquid, containerized — extent, value and cause of damage established with full recommendations to shipowners and P&I associations
  • Draft/ullage surveys for bulk cargo loaded or discharged — exact quantity determination to mitigate shortage discrepancies
  • Pre-loading surveys on specific cargo — quality and quantity investigation prior loading, assisting Master with remarks and reserves in Mate Receipt and/or Bills of Lading

Cars, Trailers & Containers

Monitoring loading and discharging operations for vehicles and containers with full damage reporting.

  • Inspection during loading and discharging operations at all Tunisian ports
  • Relative monitoring reports and damage reports issued to safeguard owners' and shippers' interests

Hull & Machinery Survey

Our surveyors and marine engineers attend hull and machinery expertise requested by P&I clubs, shipowners, or H&M underwriters.

  • Ascertaining damage from contact with quay, fixed/floating objects, grounding or collision
  • Establishing exact extent and value of damage, determining causes and liabilities
  • Recommendations for temporary or final repairs
  • Assistance with refloating/towage operations in cases of grounding, capsizing or collision

On / Off Hire Surveys

Surveys carried out for charterers and shipowners to establish the vessel's general condition at time of hire.

  • General estate of vessel documented in detail
  • Exact bunker figures, deck fittings, equipment condition and hire dates recorded
  • Full detailed report issued to the relevant party

Ship's Evaluation & Condition Survey

Evaluation Survey

Detailed report establishing the approximate value of the vessel and its equipment/fittings — prepared for purposes of possible sale or transfer.

Condition Survey

Required by P&I associations or hull underwriters prior vessel entry — establishing the overall estate of the vessel, seaworthiness, and reporting any defects or anomalies per the association's form.
